Golf has been my biggest

hobby for the past 10+ years. It all started with a nudge from my granddad, Pop, and the summer of 2015 working on a golf course. I cut pins, mowed greens & tee boxes, and raked bunkers in 90+ degree heat - then somedays I would play 9-18 holes after. Those were some 40k+ steps kind of days.

Fast forward to now, and I am still playing 9-18 holes when I can make it happen. My goals with this hobby have flucuated from having the perfect to swing to just breaking 100, 90, or 80. Right now I could shoot anywhere in that range any given day. I hope to spend more time on the course now that my degree is behind me, and I have the time play more consistently. The current goal is to break 80.

That said, having goals and working hard to reach those goals is great, but the real reason I play is for the love of the game. Golf can either be fun or torture - it's up to you which one it is for you. If golf is your hobby, golf should always be about having fun.
